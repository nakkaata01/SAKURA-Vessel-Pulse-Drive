三次元血管網を有する厚肉型培養組織の製造プロセス設計ー灌流バイオリアクターとデジタル培養制御の統合 ーProject SAKURA Ver.1.0.0

要旨 培養肉および大型三次元組織工学においては、厚みを有する組織内部への酸素・栄養供給が主要な技術ボトルネックとなっている。一般に、受動拡散のみで維持可能な細胞層の厚みは約100〜200 µmに制限されるため、数mm〜cm級の厚肉型培養組織を構築するには、灌流可能な血管様ネットワークの導入が不可欠である。  本論文では、階層型灌流ネットワーク、多段循環式バイオリアクター、動的機械刺激、およびデータ駆動型培養制御を統合した厚肉型培養組織製造プロセスの概念設計を提示する。 まず、三次元組織内部の拡散限界から出発し、1〜3 mm径の主灌流ラインと50〜300 µm径の微細灌流網から構成される階層型流路設計を示す。 ついで、筋衛星細胞、脂肪前駆細胞、内皮細胞、線維芽細胞からなる細胞構成、および電気・機械刺激を利用した筋成熟促進戦略を、食品用途に適合する形で整理する。  さらに、生体ドナー由来の環境情報や施設ごとの培養履歴と、培養組織の風味・脂質組成との関係性を体系的に扱う枠組みとして「Digital Terroir」概念を導入する。 本概念は、データ駆動型の培養条件最適化とバイオリアクター制御により、培養肉の品質プロファイルに施設固有の特徴を付与しうるという仮説に基づく。  本稿は実験データを扱うものではなく、工学的・科学的観点からの設計指針と研究ロードマップを与える概念論文である。 提案されるフレームワークは、今後の厚肉型培養組織の灌流設計およびDigital Terroirの実証研究の基盤となることを目的とする。  1. 序論 培養肉は、動物由来細胞を体外で増殖・分化させることにより食用組織を構築する技術であり、近年、試作段階から初期商業化段階へと移行しつつある。これまでの研究開発の多くは、ひき肉状やナゲット型など、比較的構造要件の緩い製品形態を対象としてきた。 一方で、ステーキ様の厚みと連続性を有する「厚肉型」培養肉では、組織工学で議論されてきた三次元構造・物質輸送・成熟の問題が本質的となる。  三次元組織内部の酸素・栄養供給は、一般に拡散距離約100〜200 µmを超えると急速に困難になるとされる。そのため、厚み方向に数mm〜cmのスケールを有する組織を安定維持するには、内部に灌流可能な血管様ネットワークを導入するか、あるいはその他の積極的な物質輸送機構を構築する必要がある。  再生医療分野では、灌流スキャフォールド、マイクロ流路ネットワーク、ホローファイバーシステムなど、様々な血管化手法が検討されてきた。しかし、食品用途における厚肉型培養組織では、以下のような追加要件が存在する。  ・食品安全性および規制要件への適合 ・成長因子や培地コストを含む経済性 ・食感・風味・見た目といった官能特性 ・生産スケールおよびプロセスの頑健性 さらに、ワインや発酵食品におけるテロワール概念に見られるように、生産環境や微生物叢が製品特性に影響を与えることが知られている。培養肉においても、生体ドナーの飼育環境や生産施設の微生物叢、培養履歴などが、組織の微細構造や代謝プロファイルに特徴的な「署名」を残しうる。  本研究の目的は、厚肉型培養組織のための階層型灌流ネットワークと灌流バイオリアクターの概念設計を提示するとともに、データ駆動型培養制御と「Digital Terroir」概念を組み合わせた新たな研究フレームワークを提案することである。
1. 【上流】オリジン・フェーズ（ドナー由来） 生命の設計図と、その個体が辿った「歴史」を定義するレイヤーです。 区分: ドナー由来情報 具体的なデータ項目: 飼料組成、温度履歴、活動量、代謝指標 視点: ここは「血統」と「育ち」をデジタル化する部分です。ワインで言えば、葡萄の品種と収穫年の気候に相当します。
2. 【中流】プラットフォーム・フェーズ（施設・環境） 生命を維持する「器」としての信頼性と、微細な環境因子を定義するレイヤーです。 区分: 施設・環境情報 具体的なデータ項目: 設備使用履歴、清掃・消毒履歴、環境センサー値 視点： 培養プロセスを汚染から守る「聖域」の証明です。HACCPを超えるレベルのトレーサビリティを担保します。  3. 【下流】エンジニアリング・フェーズ（プロセス制御） SAKURAの核心である、物理的な「肉」へと練り上げる制御レイヤーです。 区分: 培養プロセス情報 具体的なデータ項目: 流量履歴、DO・pHトレンド、刺激プロトコル
Digital Terroirは、これらの入力情報と、得られた培養組織の代謝・構造・官能特性との対応関係をデータ駆動的に抽出し、培養条件の最適化や製品プロファイルの制御に反映する試みである。 現時点では仮説段階であり、味覚再現性や消費者評価との因果関係は未検証である。  6.2 データ駆動型制御への展開 将来的には、Digital Terroirで扱う多次元データをもとに、以下のような制御戦略が考えられる。 実測データと機械学習モデルを用いた培養条件のベイズ最適化 設備・環境状態の変化を製品側の「署名」の変化として早期検知する異常検知 特定の風味・食感プロファイルを目標とした逆問題的なパラメータ探索 これらは、従来の静的レシピベースの培養から、「環境・履歴を含めた状態推定と制御」に基づくバイオコンピューティング的なアプローチへと位置づけることができる。  7. 想定性能指標と開発ロードマップ 7.1 想定性能指標 本システムの初期的な工学的目標値の一例を表に示す。 項目 初期目標 組織厚 5〜20 mm 細胞生存率 > 85% 灌流維持期間 2〜4週間 DO安定範囲 設定値±10% 流量制御誤差 設定値±5%以内 食品安全性 残留材料基準への適合 cmスケールを超えるより厚い組織や、150 mm級の大型構造体は、現時点では将来的な研究課題として位置づける。  7.2 開発ロードマップ 開発ステージは、以下の三段階に整理できる。 Phase 1：小型PoC 数mm厚の小型組織を対象に、灌流安定性と細胞生存率を中心とした概念実証を行う。 Phase 2：厚肉化と多分岐流路 厚みをcm級へと拡大し、多分岐流路による三次元灌流を実現する。テクスチャ評価など簡易な官能評価を導入し、機械刺激条件との関係を探索する。 Phase 3：生産最適化 培地コスト削減、自動制御化、スケールアップを主眼とし、Digital Terroirを含むデータ駆動型制御を本格的に導入する。  8. 技術的課題と今後の展望 現時点で想定される主要な技術的課題は以下の通りである。  高密度三次元血管網の高スループット形成 成長因子および高価培地成分のコスト抑制 酸素輸送限界および厚み方向の均一性確保 スケールアップ時の流れ場・濃度分布のばらつき 食品規制・安全性要件への適合 消費者受容性およびラベリングの在り方 特に、cm級を超える厚みを持つ組織の一様な成熟と、Digital Terroirに基づく差別化とのバランスをどこに見出すかは、科学的・社会的検討を要する。  9. 結論 本論文では、三次元血管網を有する厚肉型培養組織の製造プロセスとして、階層型灌流ネットワーク、灌流バイオリアクター、動的機械刺激、およびデータ駆動型培養制御を統合した概念設計を提示した。 また、生体ドナーおよび生産環境由来データを統合して培養条件に反映させるDigital Terroir概念を導入し、培養肉における新たな品質設計パラダイムの可能性を示した。  本稿は概念段階にとどまるものの、厚肉型培養肉の工学的設計指針と、データ駆動型バイオリアクター制御およびバイオコンピューティングの接点を明確化することで、今後の実証研究や学際的連携の出発点となることを意図している。
Manufacturing Process Design for Thick-Walled Cultured Tissue with a Three-Dimensional Vascular Network: Integration of Perfusion Bioreactor and Digital Culture Control  Project SAKURA Ver.1.0.0
Abstract In cultured meat and large-scale three-dimensional tissue engineering, oxygen and nutrient supply to the interior of thick tissues is a major technical bottleneck. Generally, the thickness of the cell layer that can be maintained by passive diffusion alone is limited to approximately 100–200 µm; therefore, the introduction of a perfusing vascular-like network is essential for constructing thick-walled cultured tissues of several millimeters to centimeters in thickness.  This paper presents a conceptual design for a thick-walled cultured tissue production process integrating a hierarchical perfusion network, a multi-stage circulating bioreactor, dynamic mechanical stimulation, and data-driven culture control. First, starting from the diffusion limits within three-dimensional tissue, a hierarchical channel design consisting of a main perfusion line with a diameter of 1–3 mm and a microperfusion network with a diameter of 50–300 µm is presented. Next, the cell composition consisting of muscle satellite cells, adipocytes, endothelial cells, and fibroblasts, and a muscle maturation promotion strategy utilizing electromechanical stimulation, are organized in a manner suitable for food applications.  Furthermore, we introduce the "Digital Terroir" concept as a framework for systematically handling the relationship between environmental information derived from living donors, facility-specific culture history, and the flavor and lipid composition of cultured tissues.  This concept is based on the hypothesis that facility-specific characteristics can be imparted to the quality profile of cultured meat through data-driven optimization of culture conditions and bioreactor control.  This paper does not deal with experimental data, but is a conceptual paper that provides design guidelines and a research roadmap from an engineering and scientific perspective.  The proposed framework aims to serve as the foundation for future perfusion design of thick-walled cultured tissues and demonstration research of Digital Terroir.  1. Introduction Cultured meat is a technology that constructs edible tissue by proliferating and differentiating animal-derived cells in vitro, and in recent years, it has been moving from the prototype stage to the early commercialization stage. Much of the research and development to date has focused on product forms with relatively loose structural requirements, such as minced meat or nugget-type products.  On the other hand, in "thick-cut" cultured meat with steak-like thickness and continuity, the issues of three-dimensional structure, substance transport, and maturation, which have been discussed in tissue engineering, become essential.  Oxygen and nutrient supply within three-dimensional tissue is generally considered to become rapidly difficult beyond a diffusion distance of approximately 100-200 µm. Therefore, to stably maintain tissue with a thickness of several millimeters to centimeters, it is necessary to introduce a perfusible vascular-like network within the tissue or to construct other active substance transport mechanisms.  In the field of regenerative medicine, various angiogenesis methods have been considered, such as perfusion scaffolds, microfluidic networks, and hollow fiber systems. However, for thick-cut cultured tissues used in food applications, the following additional requirements exist:  • Compliance with food safety and regulatory requirements • Economic viability, including growth factor and culture medium costs • Sensory characteristics such as texture, flavor, and appearance • Robustness of production scale and process Furthermore, as seen in the terroir concept in wine and fermented foods, it is known that the production environment and microbiome influence product characteristics. Even in cultured meat, the living environment of the donor, the microbiome of the production facility, and the culture history can leave a distinctive "signature" on the tissue's ultrastructure and metabolic profile.  The purpose of this study is to present a conceptual design for a hierarchical perfusion network and perfusion bioreactor for thick-walled cultured tissues, and to propose a new research framework that combines data-driven culture control with the "Digital Terroir" concept.  2. Overall System Configuration The thick-walled tissue culture production process proposed in this paper is designed as an integrated system consisting of the following elements:  Hierarchical perfusion network (main perfusion line and microperfusion network) Multistage circulating perfusion bioreactor and Life Support Unit Multicellular structure consisting of muscle, adipose, endothelial, and stromal cells Dynamic culture combining electrical and mechanical stimulation Data-driven culture control based on sensor measurements Digital Terroir integrating biological and facility-derived data Each component is described in turn below.  3. Hierarchical Perfusion Network Design 3.1 Main Perfusion Line (Macro-perfusion Channels) The main perfusion line is a relatively large-diameter channel connected to the outside of the culture cassette, and is responsible for culture medium supply, oxygen transport, and metabolite recovery. Expected inner diameter: 1–3 mm Role: Bulk transport of culture medium and distribution to internal microchannels Candidate materials: Collagen-based hydrogels, gelatin methacrylate (GelMA), biodegradable elastomers, etc. When intended for food applications, it is necessary to design the structure so that non-food polymer materials do not remain in the final product. For example, strategies such as creating a hollow structure that can be removed after culturing, or using only food-grade materials, can be considered.  3.2 Microvascular Network The microvascular network branches off from the main perfusion line and plays a role in distributing substances within the tissue. Expected channel diameter: 50–300 µm Expected function: As a capillary-like perfusion support structure, the goal is to suppress the effective diffusion distance from the cell to the channel wall to approximately 100–200 µm. Candidate Formation Methods: Sacrificial ink channel formation using the sacrificial ink method Coaxial bioprinting Stereolithography-based 3D bioprinting Since complete reproduction of biological capillary levels (5-10 µm) is difficult with current technology, this proposal defines it as an "intermediate-scale artificial channel that assists perfusion," and explores a compromise between realistic manufacturability and performance. 
4. Cell Composition and Stimulation Conditions 4.1 Cell Types Used The basic cell composition assumed for this system is as follows: Myoblasts derived from muscle satellite cells: Form the main muscle fibers Adipose progenitor cells: Contribute to lipid accumulation and flavor formation Endothelial cells: Arranged on the inner surface of the channel to improve perfusion tolerance and substance exchange efficiency Fibroblasts: Contribute to extracellular matrix formation and adjustment of tissue mechanical properties Optimization of endothelial cell coverage and arrangement density is necessary, taking into account food regulations and manufacturing costs, such as whether to aim for a complete vascular endothelial structure or limit coverage to partial coverage.  4.2 Handling of the Nervous System and Alternative Stimuli This system does not aim to replicate the biological nervous system itself. This is a design decision based on ethical and regulatory considerations for food applications, as well as manufacturing complexity. As an alternative, muscle maturation is promoted by the following external stimuli:  Electrical stimulation: Induces myotubular contraction and orientation using a fixed pattern of electrical pulses.  Periodic mechanical stretching: Stretching stimulation aimed at improving muscle fiber orientation and promoting sarcomere formation.  Low-amplitude vibration stimulation: Induces cellular responses through micro-deformation of the entire tissue.  These stimulation conditions need to be optimized considering food safety and scale-up potential, while evaluating their impact on muscle properties and texture.  5. Dynamic Culture and Life Support Unit 5.1 Perfusion Control and Sensor Measurement Low-shear pumps (e.g., peristaltic pump, diaphragm micropump) are used for circulating culture medium into the tissue. The controlled parameters are as follows:  Flow Rate Pressure Difference (ΔP) Dissolved Oxygen (DO) pH Glucose and Lactate Concentration These sensor values are acquired in real time, and perfusion conditions are dynamically adjusted through feedback control.  5.2 Shear Stress Control Excessive flow velocity causes cell damage and structural disruption; therefore, especially in the presence of endothelial cells, the goal is to maintain the shear stress acting on the endothelial surface at approximately 0.5–2 Pa. This range is based on general guidelines in existing vascular tissue engineering research.  5.3 Integration of Mechanical Stimulation In addition to the electrical stimulation described above, the following mechanical stimuli are integrated within the bioreactor: Periodic stretching stimulation: By stretching the tissue at a constant period, the orientation and maturation of muscle fibers are promoted. Low-amplitude vibration stimulation: Influences contact between muscle fibers and matrix formation, aiming for final texture improvement. Quantifying the relationship between these stimulation parameters and the final texture and mechanical properties will be a future research topic.  5.4 Life Support Unit (LSU) The external Life Support Unit is a unit responsible for purifying the culture medium and replenishing its components, and is envisioned to perform the following functions: Removal targets: Metabolic by-products such as lactic acid and ammonia Replenishment targets: Glucose, amino acids, growth factors, electrolytes While a closed-loop system is the basic circulation method, complete recycling of growth factors and high-value components is not realistic, so operation combining partial bypass exchange and concentrated fractionation is envisioned.  6. Data-Driven Culture Control and Digital Terroir 6.1 Digital Terroir Concept This paper proposes "Digital Terroir" as a framework for integrating information on living donors, environmental information of production facilities, and culture conditions/bioreactor behavior. Examples of typical input information handled here are shown in the table.  1. [Upstream] Origin Phase (Donor Origin): This layer defines the blueprint of life and the "history" that the individual has followed. Category: Donor Origin Information Specific Data Items: Feed composition, temperature history, activity level, metabolic indicators Perspective: This is where "bloodline" and "upbringing" are digitized. In wine terms, this corresponds to the grape variety and the climate of the harvest year.  2. [Midstream] Platform Phase (Facilities/Environment): This layer defines the reliability of the "vessel" that sustains life and the minute environmental factors. Category: Facility/Environment Information Specific Data Items: Equipment usage history, cleaning/disinfection history, environmental sensor values Perspective: This is proof of a "sanctuary" that protects the cultivation process from contamination. It guarantees traceability at a level exceeding HACCP.  3. [Downstream] Engineering Phase (Process Control): This is the control layer that refines SAKURA into its core physical "meat."  Category: Culture Process Information Specific Data Items: Flow Rate History, DO/pH Trends, Stimulation Protocol  Digital Terroir is an attempt to extract the correspondence between this input information and the metabolic, structural, and sensory characteristics of the obtained cultured tissue in a data-driven manner, and to reflect this in the optimization of culture conditions and the control of product profiles.  Currently, this is in the hypothesis stage, and the causal relationship with taste reproducibility and consumer evaluation has not yet been verified.  6.2 Development into Data-Driven Control In the future, based on the multidimensional data handled by Digital Terroir, the following control strategies can be considered: Bayesian optimization of culture conditions using measured data and machine learning models Anomaly detection that detects changes in equipment and environmental conditions early as changes in the product's "signature" Inverse problem-based parameter search targeting specific flavor and texture profiles These can be positioned as a biocomputation-based approach based on "state estimation and control including environment and history," rather than the conventional static recipe-based culture.  7. Expected Performance Indicators and Development Roadmap 7.1 Expected Performance Indicators An example of the initial engineering target values for this system is shown in the table. Item Initial Target Tissue Thickness 5-20 mm Cell Viability > 85% Perfusion Maintenance Period 2-4 weeks DO Stability Range ±10% of Setpoint Flow Rate Control Error Within ±5% of Setpoint Food Safety Compliance with Residual Material Standards Tissues thicker than the cm scale and large structures of 150 mm class are currently positioned as future research topics.  7.2 Development Roadmap The development stages can be organized into the following three stages. Phase 1: Small-Scale PoC Conceptual proof focusing on perfusion stability and cell viability will be conducted using small tissues with a thickness of several mm. Phase 2: Thickening and Multi-Branched Channels The thickness will be expanded to the cm class, and three-dimensional perfusion will be realized using multi-branched channels. Simple sensory evaluations such as texture evaluation will be introduced to explore the relationship with mechanical stimulation conditions. Phase 3: Production Optimization Focusing on reducing culture medium costs, automating control, and scaling up, this phase will fully implement data-driven control, including Digital Terroir.  8. Technical Challenges and Future Prospects The main technical challenges anticipated at this time are as follows:  High-throughput formation of high-density three-dimensional vascular networks Cost reduction of growth factors and expensive culture medium components Ensuring oxygen transport limits and uniformity in the thickness direction Variations in flow field and concentration distribution during scale-up Compliance with food regulations and safety requirements Consumer acceptability and labeling methods In particular, finding the balance between uniform maturation of tissues with thicknesses exceeding several centimeters and differentiation based on Digital Terroir requires scientific and social consideration.  9. Conclusion This paper presents a conceptual design integrating a hierarchical perfusion network, perfusion bioreactor, dynamic mechanical stimulation, and data-driven culture control as a manufacturing process for thick-walled cultured tissues with a three-dimensional vascular network.  Furthermore, we introduced the Digital Terroir concept, which integrates data from living donors and the production environment and reflects it in the culture conditions, demonstrating the potential of a new quality design paradigm in cultured meat.  Although this paper remains at a conceptual stage, it aims to clarify the connection between engineering design guidelines for thick-cut cultured meat and data-driven bioreactor control and biocomputing, thereby serving as a starting point for future demonstration research and interdisciplinary collaborations.
